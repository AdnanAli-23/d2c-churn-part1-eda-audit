# Business Memo

## Executive Summary

An exploratory churn analysis was conducted on 2,400 customers to identify factors associated with customer churn.

The overall churn rate was approximately 47%, indicating a significant retention challenge.

The analysis identified several strong behavioral patterns that can be used to improve retention strategies and reduce customer attrition.

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Customer Inactivity is the Strongest Churn Indicator

Customers inactive for 31–60 days showed an 88.36% churn rate.

Customers inactive for only 0–7 days showed a churn rate of 22.20%.

### Recommendation

Implement automated re-engagement campaigns for customers who have not visited for more than 15 days.

---

## 2. Website Engagement Strongly Reduces Churn

Customers with low engagement showed a churn rate of 61.29%.

Customers with very high engagement showed a churn rate of only 18.65%.

### Recommendation

Increase customer engagement through personalized recommendations, content campaigns, and loyalty incentives.

---

## 3. Purchase Frequency Improves Retention

Customers with low order frequency showed a churn rate of 72.81%.

Customers with very high order frequency showed a churn rate of only 15.81%.

### Recommendation

Encourage repeat purchases through targeted promotions and loyalty programs.

---

## 4. Higher Spending Customers are More Loyal

Low-spending customers showed a churn rate of 77.33%.

Very high-spending customers showed a churn rate of only 24.00%.

### Recommendation

Identify and nurture high-value customers while increasing spending among lower-value segments.

---

## 5. Loyalty Programs Improve Retention

Silver customers experienced the highest churn rates.

Platinum customers experienced the lowest churn rates.

### Recommendation

Create upgrade incentives that encourage customers to move into higher loyalty tiers.

---

## 6. Acquisition Channels Differ in Customer Quality

Organic and Referral customers demonstrated lower churn compared with paid acquisition channels.

### Recommendation

Increase investment in higher-retention acquisition sources.

---

## Secondary Findings

- Age group showed limited impact on churn.
- Marketing consent showed minimal relationship with churn.
- Abandoned cart behavior did not behave as initially expected.
- Support ticket reopening showed weak association with churn.
- Return behavior showed limited predictive value.

---

# Priority Actions

## High Priority

1. Reduce customer inactivity.
2. Improve website engagement.
3. Increase repeat purchasing behavior.

## Medium Priority

4. Expand loyalty program participation.
5. Improve customer onboarding experience.
6. Optimize acquisition channel allocation.

## Low Priority

7. Age-based targeting.
8. Marketing consent segmentation.

---

# Conclusion

The strongest churn signals are behavioral rather than demographic.

Customer inactivity, engagement levels, spending behavior, and purchase frequency should be prioritized when designing retention strategies.

Future work should focus on predictive modeling to quantify the combined impact of these drivers and identify customers at highest risk of churn.
